#4 - STIR FRIED BEEF HO FEN - 乾炒牛河

I learned how to make this dish from a professional chef-training course. The myth is that it needs lots of oil to make this dish good. However, in fact, the key is to heat up the wok/frying pan hot before putting the oil. In the restaurant, the beef is cooked by “run through” in a pot of oil (走油) before mixing with the noodle. This is a very common technic for restaurant cooking. However, I blanch the meat in water, which is less oily and easy to execute for home cooking.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 375g Ho Fen河粉 (fresh thick rice noodle)
  • 75g beef, sliced
  • Onion, yellow chive, bean sprout (

BEEF MARINADE:

  • 1 TBSP oil
  • Pinch of each salt, baking soda, chicken powder (optional)
  • 2 TBSP (or more) water

MAIN SEASONING:

  • 1TBSP soy sauce
  • 1TBSP dark soy sauce
  • 1 tsp oyster sauce
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp chicken powder (optional)
  • ¼ tsp Maggie sauce

Directions:

  1. Mix beef with beef marinade and set aside.
  2. Slice onion and cut yellow chive into 1 ½ inch long.
  3. Boil a small pot of water; put the beef in, stir.
  4. As soon as the beef is cooked (turn from red to brownish), drain and set aside.
  5. Heat up the wok/large frying pan. Add 2 TBSP oil to the wok after the wok is hot.
  6. Put in the onion, and then add in Ho Fen and bean sprout. Stir-fry until the Ho Fen is soft and the onion is translucent.
  7. Add beef and seasoning. Mix well. Then finally add the yellow chive.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • A pinch is third of ¼ tsp, which is about ½ mil. Therefore, it is just the slightest amount that you pinch between two fingers.
  • If you buy the fresh Ho Fen noodle, but not cook this dish the same day. You should shake loose the noodle, before putting into the refrigerator. Otherwise, the noodle will stick together and hard to separate when hardens.
