#6 - SMOKE FISH - 燻魚

This is one of the signature Shanghainese dish. I am not bragging, my Mom’s version is better than most of the restaurants. Though the recipe is simple and the steps are easy, though the deep-frying is bit challenging for most people. The key is to control the splatter.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 Catty (600g) Carp 鯇魚
  • 1 TBSP Shao Xin wine
  • 4 TBSP Dark soy sauce
  • 3 TBSP Sugar
  • 1 Green onion
  • Oil for frying

Directions:

  1. Cut the carp in 3/4in. steak. Rinse clean with cool water.
  2. Before frying, pat dry the fish thoroughly with paper towel or kitchen towel.
  3. Mix the Shao Xin wine, soy sauce, sugar in a mixing bowl. Break the green onion into 2 or 3 pieces and add into the bowl to form dipping sauce.
  4. Heat the oil in a deep frying pan until hot.
  5. Pat dry the fish one more time before put a few pieces of fish at a time into the frying oil. (Leave some space between the pieces, so they do not stick together).
  6. Cover the frying pan with a spatter guard, or a lid if needed to protect yourself from spatter.
  7. Let it fry for 1 min before flipping the fish. Fry both side of the fish until lightly brown.
  8. Remove the fish from the hot oil and dip it into the dipping sauce immediate. Make sure the whole piece is well coated with the dipping sauce.
  9. Put the fish on a serving plate.
  10. Repeat steps 5-9 until all the fish is done.
  11. Pour the remaining sauce over the fish and marinate for at least 2 hours before serving.
  12. Serve at room temperature.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • You know the oil is hot enough for frying when putting a chopstick or a small piece of bread, the oil starts steadily bubbling.
  • Ways to reduce spatter during deep-frying are: very dry ingredient to reduce surface water; use a deep frying pan to contain spatter, use spatter guard or lid to prevent spatter to excape.
