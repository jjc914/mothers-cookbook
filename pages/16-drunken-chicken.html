#16 - DRUNKEN CHICKEN花 - 雕醉雞

This is an authentic Shanghainese dish as well as my mother’s signature dish. It is a must for her dinner parties and the guests loved it. It is better to use Huadiao 花雕 (the aged and better quality of Shaoxing wine) 紹興酒. This will give the chicken a strong wine fragrant and mellow taste. This dish is served as one of the many appetizers for a Shanghainese banquet and can prepared 1-2 days ahead of the time.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 whole chicken (approx. 2 pounds)
  • 2 cups Haodiao花雕
  • 6 TBSP sugar
  • 1 green onion
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Clean and pat dry chicken with paper towel.
  2. Rub inside and outside of chicken with salt and pepper.
  3. Put 2 slices of ginger and 1 green onion in the cavity.
  4. Steam chicken for 45 minutes or until well cooked. Dip into cold water to stop cooking.
  5. When the chicken cooled enough to handle, cut it into quarters.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, mix wine, sugar, salt and one green onion for marinating sauce.
  7. Submerge the chicken pieces in the marinating sauce.
  8. Marinate it for 1 day in the refrigerator. Turn chicken from time to time to submerge into the sauce.
  9. Take the chicken out of the marinade and cut into pieces. Arrange onto serving plate.
  10. Pour some marinating sauce on the chicken and serve at room.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • To test if the chicken is properly cooked, poke a chopstick into the thigh. It is done if and the juice comes out has no red color.
  • If you can substitute whole chicken with 2 pounds of chicken wings or other cuts of chicken. Steaming time for chicken wings will be 20 minutes.
  • You may use Shaoxing wine to substitute Huadiao.
