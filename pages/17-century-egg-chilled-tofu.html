#17 - CHILLED TOFU WITH CENTURY EGG - 涼拌皮蛋豆腐

Century egg is duck egg that is preserved in clay, salt and rice husk for a few weeks to months. The egg whites turns to dark jelly like and the yolk is grey creamy and succulent. It is a Chinese delicacy that many foreigners do not have the stomach for it. Toon leaves is an unusual vegetable from Eastern China. It has a unique and pungent flavor. It is commonly found in the preserved form. It is ok to omit it from this recipe.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 pack soft bean curd
  • 1 Thousand-year old egg
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • Green onion
  • 1 pc 香椿菜 (preserved toon leaves) optional
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp chicken powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • ½ tsp sugar

Directions:

  1. Peel the thousand-year old egg and cut into small pieces. Set aside.
  2. Chop香椿菜and soak in a bowl of water for at least 30 min. Rinse in water to remove the saltiness. Put it in a bowl.
  3. Peel and minced the garlic.
  4. In a small frying pan, heat 2TBSP oil, put in the minced garlic and fry until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  5. Pour the remaining hot oil onto the toon, so it make a sizzling sound.
  6. Cut the green onion into small pieces.
  7. Cut tofu into ¾-inches pieces. Mix with salt and chicken powder.
  8. Put it in the microwave oven for 5 minutes.
  9. Drain off the excessive liquid and let cool.
  10. Mix soy sauce, sugar and sesame oil.
  11. Arrange tofu on the bottom, minced garlic, toon on the top. Garnish with chopped green onion.
  12. Pour the sauce on top, mix and serve.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • You may opt out the century egg and the preserved toon leaves, and even the fried minced garlic if you prefer. Just start the recipe from step 6.
