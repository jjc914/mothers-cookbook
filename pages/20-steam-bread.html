#20 - STEAM BREAD - 發麵夾餅

This is a Cantonese version of steam bread. It uses both yeast and baking powder. I find this way of making steam bread not only it is easy and time efficient, but also yields more consistent result than the yeast alone version. This bread is a good accompany with honey-glazed ham蜜汁火腿 and braised pork belly 紅燒肉.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 160g cake (low gluten) flour
  • 90g lukewarm water
  • 12g sugar
  • ½ tsp (2g) yeast
  • 1tsp (4g) baking powder

Directions:

  1. Mix yeast, water, and let stand for 1 minute. You will see the yeast foaming up. That means that the yeast is alive and active.
  2. Mix cake flour, sugar and baking flour. Pour the yeast and water mixture into the dry ingredient.
  3. Mix well, and then knead by hand until the dough is smooth and a bit shiny on the outside.
  4. Test to see if the dough is ready by cutting it with a knife. The dough is ready if the cut surface is smooth.
  5. Roll the dough into a long tube, and cut it into 12 equal portions.
  6. Turn the cut surface facing up, and press down each piece to form a circular shape.
  7. Sprinkle some flour onto each piece and onto the counter top.
  8. Use rolling pin to roll the dough out to a long oval shape about 2” x 4” x ¼ inch thick.
  9. Brush some vegetable oil on the surface and fold it in half. Place it on a pre-cut 2”x2” wax paper.
  10. Repeat step 8 & 9 for all pieces. Place finished bread in a steamer, put the steamer in a warm place away from draft (eg. inside an oven), and let it rise for 30-40 minutes.
  11. Heat water in a wok until boiling and place the steamer on top and steam for 10 minutes.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • Makes 12 steam bread approximately 2”x2” in size.
  • For best result, use cake flour, but you may substitute with all-purpose flour as wish.
  • Knead by pushing the dough out away from your body with a long stroke. Then fold the dough in and repeat.
  • Step 10, 11 – you may put the finished bread on a cookie sheet lined with wax paper, and then cover it with kitchen towel to let it rise. Move the bread into a steamer when ready to cook.
