#22 - PRESERVED VEGETABLE WITH PORK - 雪裡紅炒肉絲

This dish goes well by itself, but particularly well with soup noodle or stir-fried Shanghainese rice stick. It is also a proper dish in Shanghainese restaurants.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 100 g Salted preserved vegetable (雪裡紅)
  • 150g Pork slices
  • 100g can / fresh Bamboo shoot
  • ½ Red bell pepper
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 TBSP Cooking wine
  • Vegetable oil

Marinade:

  • 2 slices ginger
  • ½ tsp salt
  • pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp cornstarch / potato starch
  • 3 TBSP water

Directions:

  1. Cut the salted preserved vegetable (雪裡紅) into small pieces. Cut the leave in both directions, so it is not stringy.
  2. Soak the vegetable in a bowl of water for at least 4 hours. Change water a few times to wash out the saltiness.
  3. Squeeze dry the salted vegetable and put 1 tsp of sugar to marinate.
  4. Slice bamboo shoot into thin julienne along the fiber, and then boil it in some salt water to remove the canned taste.
  5. Slice pork into fine julienne then put in the marinade ingredients. Mix well and let sit for ½ hour.
  6. Slice the red bell pepper into the same julienne size.
  7. In a wok or frying pan, add 2 TBSP vegetable oil.
  8. When oil is hot, add the preserved vegetable. Stir-fry for a few minutes in low heat. Make sure it does not burn.
  9. Add the bamboo shoot then a few TBSP water. Cook for another 1 – 2 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  10. Clean the wok or frying pan then put on the heat again. When hot, add some vegetable oil.
  11. Put the pork in and stir-fry for a bit. Add the Shao Xing wine.
  12. When the pork is fully cooked, add the preserved vegetable, mix well. Just before serving, add the red bell pepper.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • Easier way to slice pork: Put the fresh pork in the freezer a day before using it. Remove from freezer and slightly defrost it (1 hour) for easy slicing. The pork should be solid, but easy for the knife to slice through.
  • You may prepare this dish early or when free, and add it to soup noodle, fried noodle or fried rice cake when you need.
  • Chicken maybe used instead of pork.
