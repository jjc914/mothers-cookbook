#30 - PAN-FRIED PORK BUN - 生煎包

This is a very popular Shanghainese dim sum. The Chinese name生煎包, which literally translated to “raw pan-fried bun”. You are supposed to pan-fry the raw buns to golden crispy on bottom, soft on the top and juicy inside. Unfortunately, most restaurants pre-steam the buns before gently brown the bottom before serving which yields an inferior dish.

INGREDIENTS:

    Dough:

    • 160g cake (low gluten) flour
    • 1 TBSP sugar
    • 4g (1 tsp) baking powder
    • 90ml warm water (45ºC)
    • 2g ½ tsp yeast

    Stuffing:

    • 120g minced pork
    • 1 tsp Grated ginger
    • 1 Green onion, chopped

    Seasoning:

    • ¼ tsp salt
    • ¼ tsp Shaoxing wine
    • ½ tsp dark soy sauce
    • 1 TBSP potato starch
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • White pepper
    • 4 TBSP water
    • 2 tsp vegetable oil
    • ½ tsp sesame oil

    Garnish:

    • Toasted sesame seed
    • Chopped green onion

Directions:

  1. Put the yeast into the warm water. Let it sit for 5 minutes to activate.
  2. Put all the ingredients in stuffing into a bowl; add in all the seasoning (except the water, oil and sesame oil).
  3. Using chopsticks, stir the minced meat vigorously in circular motion until it becomes sticky pasty consistence.
  4. Add in 1 TBSP of water into the meat, stir vigorously again in circular motion until it becomes sticky pasty consistence.
  5. Repeat step 4 until all the water is incorporated into the meat.
  6. Add in the vegetable oil; mix gently, then the sesame oil. Set aside.
  7. Mix cake flour, sugar and baking flour in a bowl. Pour in the yeast and water mixture.
  8. Mix well, and then knead by hand until the dough is smooth and a bit shiny on the outside(about 3-5min).
  9. Test to see if the dough is ready by cutting it with a knife. The dough is ready if the cut surface is smooth.
  10. Divide the dough into 12 equal portions (approx. 20g each).
  11. Press each piece flat. Use a rolling pin, roll the dough out into a 3 ½ - 4 inch circle.
  12. Roughly, divide the meat stuffing into 12 equal portions by drawing lines in it.
  13. Put one portion of the meat onto a rolled-out dough. While turning the dough, squeeze it close, leaving no opening on the top. Repeat for the rest of the dough.
  14. Put the finished bun on a floured baking sheet and cover with a towel. Put the baking sheet in a warm place for 30-40 min to let it rise.
  15. Heat a frying pan (preferably cast iron) with ¾ cup water and 1 TBSP oil. When the water boils, line the buns in a single layer on the frying pan without touching each other.
  16. Cover the lid and let it cook for 7-8 minutes.
  17. Uncover the lid and let the excessive water evaporate.
  18. Add another 2 TBSP oil between the buns.
  19. It is done when all the water is evaporated and the bottom of the bun is golden brown.
  20. Put the buns onto a serving plate. Garnish with toasted sesame seed and chopped green onion.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • Can use all-purpose flour if you don’t have cake flour.
  • The yeast is alive if it foams up after a few minutes in the water.
  • Serve with your favorite hot sauce, Zhenjiang vinegar鎮江醋, or Worcestershire sauce.
