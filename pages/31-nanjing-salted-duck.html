#31 - NANJING SALTED DUCK - 南京鹽水鴨

Nanjing is regarded as the duck capital of China. The origin of Nanjing salted duck dated back to 14th century. The meat of this duck is tender, tastes lightly salty, but not greasy. I learnt how to cook this dish from my cousin on my mother’s side.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 duck (1.5-2kg)
  • 6 TBSP salt
  • 2 Star anise
  • 20g ginger
  • 2 green onion
  • ¼ cup Shaoxing wine

Directions:

  1. Wash the duck in cold water. Using a tweezer to remove excessive hair on the duck. Pat dry.
  2. Heat the salt and the star anise on low heat in a frying pan until the aroma comes out. Turn off and let it cool.
  3. Rub the salt mixture in and out of the duck and put it in the refrigerator for 24 hours.
  4. Remove from the refrigerator, put the ginger and green onion into the cavity of the duck.
  5. Put the duck in a big pot breast side down. Cover the duck with water. Add in Shaoxing wine.
  6. Bring the pot to boil and let it boil for 20 minutes in medium heat. Make sure that the water does not boil over and the duck is submerged in the water.
  7. Turn down the heat and let it simmer for another 20 minutes.
  8. Remove the duck from the liquid and let cool.
  9. Chop up into pieces and serve.

(Serves 1 person)
