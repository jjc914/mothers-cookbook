#35 - BRAISED PORK BELLY IN SOY SAUCE - 紅燒肉

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1½ catty (900g) pork belly
  • 50 g rock sugar (broken up to smaller pieces)
  • 2 TBSP light soy sauce
  • 2 TBSP dark soy sauce
  • 2 TBSP Shao Xing wine
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 pc Chinese cinnamon
  • 2 TBSP oil
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 1 green onion

Directions:

  1. Wash and cut the pork belly into 1x1 inch pieces.
  2. Blanch the pork belly in boiling water for 2 minutes with some cooking wine and ginger.
  3. Rinse the pork belly in cold water and wash off the scums.
  4. In a wok, heat 2 TBSP oil. When the oil is hot, put in the pork belly. Add in the star anise, Chinese cinnamon, 2 slices ginger, and 1 green onion.
  5. Stir fry until the pork is slightly brown and the aroma of the spices comes out.
  6. Remove the pork belly and the spices completely from the wok but leaving the oil inside.
  7. Add in the rock sugar. Stir continuously in low flame. Cook until the sugar melts and turn light brown.
  8. Return the pork and the spices into the wok. Add in the soy sauces, wine and 2 cups of water.
  9. Bring it to boil and simmer for 1 hour.
  10. Condense the sauce. There should be a shining dark glace on the pork and almost no sauce left.
  11. Serve with steam bread.

(Serves 1 person)

Note:
  • Option to add bean curd knots (百葉結) into the pork: Cook bean knots in broth, or water with oil and salt for 10 minutes, then drain. Combine with the pork before step 10.
