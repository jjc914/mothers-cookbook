#1 - EASY COLD NOODLES - 涼麵

My mom used to make me this dish when I was young as a school packed lunch. It is simple and nutritious. My classmates used to be very jealous of yummy home lunch. I wrote this recipe for my old classmate a few years ago when we recollected our childhood memories at a re-union.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 instant noodle / 60g any kind of noodle
  • 1 egg
  • 1 slice cooked ham / some cooked chicken
  • 2 kinds of vegetable (1 tomato, 1/2 cucumber, 1 carrot...)

SAUCE:

  • 1 TBSP soy sauce
  • 1 TBSP vinegar
  • 1 TBSP cold water
  • 1/2 TBSP sugar
  • Sesame oil
  • White pepper

Directions:

  1. Beat egg is a bowl. Put some oil in a frying pan. Heat up the oil, add the egg in and scramble it. Remove the cooked egg onto a chopping board.
  2. Put some water into the pot and bring to boil. Put the noodle into the pot and cook until done. Remove and rinse in cold water.
  3. Put a little bit salt and vegetable oil onto the noodle and toss. Arrange the noodle on a serving plate.
  4. On the chopping board, cut the scramble egg into small pieces. Place on the noodle.
  5. Cut ham or chicken into julienne. Place onto the noodle.
  6. Cut the vegetables into fine julienne and put onto the noodle.
  7. Put all ingredients for the sauce into a small bowl and mix well.
  8. Pour the sauce onto the noodle and serve. Add your favorite hot sauce to the noodle for additional flavor.
(Serve 1 person)

Note:

You can use any fresh vegetable you like (raw or lightly blanched). Here are some that I like to use sometimes: bell pepper, bean sprout, snap peas, or even mango...
#2 - STEAM MARINATED CHICKEN WITH PEPPERCORN - 花椒醃雞 This is a spin-off of the more famous traditional dish Nanjing salted duck鹽水鴨. This recipe uses chicken, which is more readily available, and a simpler cooking method. Nonetheless, the finished product is just as delicious as its famous cousin.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 whole chicken (approx. 3 lb.)
  • 2 TBSP salt
  • 1 TBSP Sichuan peppercorn

Directions:

  1. Toast salt and Sichuan peppercorn in a frying pan with low heat. Move the frying pan in circular motion constantly until the smell of the peppercorn comes out.
  2. Rub peppercorn salt mixture on the surface and inside of the chicken. Cover and marinate for 1-3 day in the refrigerator. (the longer it marinade, the saltier it becomes)
  3. Before steaming, remove the peppercorn from the surface of the chicken and put it in a deep dish.
  4. Heat up at least 2 cup of water in the big steamer. Let the water boil, then put the chicken in and steam for 45 min - 1 hour.
  5. Let it cool down until it can be handled by hand.
  6. Tear meat off bones and arrange bone at the bottom, meat and skin on top.
  7. Alternatively, you can chop the chicken into pieces and arrange on a serving plate.
  8. Garnish with chopped green onion.

Note:

  1. To test if the chicken is properly cooked, poke a chopstick into the thigh. It is done if and the juice comes out has no red color.
  2. You can substitute with chicken thigh or breast instead if prefer.
#3 - SWEET AND SOUR CUCUMBER SALAD - 糖醋青瓜 My mother loved to cook and she regularly invited relatives and friends over for dinner party. She often made this dish as one of the many appetizers. It can be prepare one – two days ahead of the time, so no hassle at the day of the party.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 Cucumber (12 inches long each)
  • ½ Red bell pepper
  • 3 TBSP White vinegar
  • 3 TBSP Sugar
  • 1 TBSP Sichuan peppercorn
  • 2 cloves Garlic
  • ½ tsp salt

Directions:

  1. Wash cucumber, cut into 1/2 lengthwise and remove the seeds.
  2. Cut into 2-inch-long sections and each section into 3 thin strip.
  3. Add salt in and marinate for 2 hours.
  4. Drain and squeeze the cucumber to get rid of excess water.
  5. Toast the Sichuan peppercorn in a frying pan on low heat until aromatic.
  6. Slice the red bell pepper into the approximate size of the cucumber. Slice the garlic into small pieces.
  7. Put all ingredients into a glass jar and mix well. Refrigerate for at least 1 day before serving.
  8. Pick out peppercorns before serving.
#4 - STIR FRIED BEFF HO FEN - 乾炒牛河 I learned how to make this dish from a professional chef-training course. The myth is that it needs lots of oil to make this dish good. However, in fact, the key is to heat up the wok/frying pan hot before putting the oil. In the restaurant, the beef is cooked by “run through” in a pot of oil (走油) before mixing with the noodle. This is a very common technic for restaurant cooking. However, I blanch the meat in water, which is less oily and easy to execute for home cooking.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 375g Ho Fen河粉 (fresh thick rice noodle)
  • 75g beef, sliced
  • Onion, yellow chive, bean sprout (

BEEF MARINADE:

  • 1 TBSP oil
  • Pinch of each salt, baking soda, chicken powder (optional)
  • 2 TBSP (or more) water

MAIN SEASONING:

  • 1TBSP soy sauce
  • 1TBSP dark soy sauce
  • 1 tsp oyster sauce
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp chicken powder (optional)
  • ¼ tsp Maggie sauce

Directions:

  1. Mix beef with beef marinade and set aside.
  2. Slice onion and cut yellow chive into 1 ½ inch long.
  3. Boil a small pot of water; put the beef in, stir.
  4. As soon as the beef is cooked (turn from red to brownish), drain and set aside.
  5. Heat up the wok/large frying pan. Add 2 TBSP oil to the wok after the wok is hot.
  6. Put in the onion, and then add in Ho Fen and bean sprout. Stir-fry until the Ho Fen is soft and the onion is translucent.
  7. Add beef and seasoning. Mix well. Then finally add the yellow chive.

Note:

  1. A pinch is third of ¼ tsp, which is about ½ mil. Therefore, it is just the slightest amount that you pinch between two fingers.
  2. If you buy the fresh Ho Fen noodle, but not cook this dish the same day. You should shake loose the noodle, before putting into the refrigerator. Otherwise, the noodle will stick together and hard to separate when hardens.
#5 - NOODLE WITH MINCED PORK BEAN SAUCE- 炸醬麵 This is a favorite lunch in my household. I make it almost every week. When we sit down to eat, each of us will has our own personal hot sauce with different flavor and degree of spiciness.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 cucumber
  • 200g dry noodle
  • 200g minced pork
  • 1-Cup chopped vegetable (onion, carrot, celery string beans etc.)
  • 1 TBSP cooking wine
  • 1 TBSP potato starch (or corn starch) mix with ¼ Cup water for thickening

SAUCE:

  • 2TBSP dark soy sauce
  • 1 ½ TBSP bean paste
  • 1 TBSP hoi sin sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 180ml water

Directions:

  1. Cook the noodle per package instruction.
  2. Cut the cucumber into fine julienne. Set aside.
  3. Heat up 2 TBSP oil in frying pan, put in the minced pork.
  4. Sauté the minced pork for a few min, add cooking wine. Remove and set aside.
  5. Add a bit more oil in the frying pan, put in the chopped vegetable and sauté for a few minutes.
  6. Add in the meat, and all the ingredients for the sauce.
  7. Bring to boil, and then let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Add the potato starch mixture while stirring to thicken the sauce.
  9. Put noodle in a bowl, top with minced pork and cucumber.
  10. Serve with personal favorite hot sauce.
#6 - SMOKE FISH - 燻魚 This is one of the signature Shanghainese dish. I am not bragging, my Mom’s version is better than most of the restaurants. Though the recipe is simple and the steps are easy, though the deep-frying is bit challenging for most people. The key is to control the splatter.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 Catty (600g) Carp 鯇魚
  • 1 TBSP Shao Xin wine
  • 4 TBSP Dark soy sauce
  • 3 TBSP Sugar
  • 1 Green onion
  • Oil for frying

Directions:

  1. Cut the carp in 3/4in. steak. Rinse clean with cool water.
  2. Before frying, pat dry the fish thoroughly with paper towel or kitchen towel.
  3. Mix the Shao Xin wine, soy sauce, sugar in a mixing bowl. Break the green onion into 2 or 3 pieces and add into the bowl to form dipping sauce.
  4. Heat the oil in a deep frying pan until hot.
  5. Pat dry the fish one more time before put a few pieces of fish at a time into the frying oil. (Leave some space between the pieces, so they do not stick together).
  6. Cover the frying pan with a spatter guard, or a lid if needed to protect yourself from spatter.
  7. Let it fry for 1 min before flipping the fish. Fry both side of the fish until lightly brown.
  8. Remove the fish from the hot oil and dip it into the dipping sauce immediate. Make sure the whole piece is well coated with the dipping sauce.
  9. Put the fish on a serving plate.
  10. Repeat steps 5-9 until all the fish is done.
  11. Pour the remaining sauce over the fish and marinate for at least 2 hours before serving.
  12. Serve at room temperature.

Note:

  1. You know the oil is hot enough for frying when putting a chopstick or a small piece of bread, the oil starts steadily bubbling.
  2. Ways to reduce spatter during deep-frying are: very dry ingredient to reduce surface water; use a deep frying pan to contain spatter, use spatter guard or lid to prevent spatter to excape.
#7 - PEKING STYLE FRIED TOFU - 鍋鎉豆腐 This is my favorite Tofu dish. I cook it regularly at home and for dinner parties.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 packs of firm Tofu
  • ½ Cup flour
  • 1-2 egg
  • 1 TBSP chopped green onion
  • 1 TBSP chopped ginger root
  • 1 TBSP zhaoxing wine
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ TBSP sesame oil
  • ½ Cup chicken / vegetable stock
  • Vegetable oil for pan-frying

Directions:

  1. Beat the egg and add salt; cut the tofu into approx. 2 ½ x 1 x ½ inches pieces.
  2. Coat each pieces lightly with flour, and line them up single layer on a plate.
  3. Put 2 TBSP oil into a frying pan. While waiting for the oil to heat up, dip a few pieces of tofu into egg mixture.
  4. When the oil is hot, arrange one layer of the tofu in the frying pan.
  5. Fry over medium heat until golden brown, turn over and fry the other side to golden brown. Remove from pan.
  6. Repeat #2-4 until all the pieces are done.
  7. Pour out the excess oil (if there is any), add ginger into frying pan and sauté until the aroma comes out.
  8. Return all the tofu into the frying pan add the wine, chicken stock; pierce bean curd with a fork to allow liquid to soak through.
  9. Cook over low flame until liquid is absorbed by the tofu.
  10. Sprinkle the green onion in and arrange it on a serving plate.
  11. Add sesame oil and serve.

Note:

Of course, I eat this dish with some of my favorite hot sauce.
#8 - CHICKEN AND MUNG BEAN SHEET SALAD - 雞絲粉皮 You can find this dish in all Shanghainese restaurant menu because everyone likes it. Though the name of this dish is Chicken and Mung Bean Sheet Salad, it is totally fine to skip the chicken. That will make it vegetarian and vegan dish.

  • 100 g cooked chicken meat
  • 1 cucumber
  • 200 g fresh mung bean sheet
  • 1 green onion
  • 1 tsp roasted sesame seed
  • 375g Ho Fen河粉 (fresh thick rice noodle)

SAUCE:

  • 2 TBSP sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 clove garlic, finely minced
  • 1 TBSP Zhenjiang vinegar鎮江醋
  • 1 TBSP light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 TBSP water / chicken broth
  • 1 TBSP oil
  • 4 TBSP sesame paste

Directions:

  1. Wash and cut the cucumber into fine julienne. Set aside.
  2. Cut the mung bean sheet into ¼ to ½-inch strips.

  2. If you can find fresh mung bean sheet, you may use the dry version. Cook it in boiling water just like noodle, and then rinse it in cold water.
#9 - MANGO COCONUT GLUTINOUS RICE - 椰汁芒果鴛鴦糯米

INGREDIENTS:

  • 100g (1/2 cup) black glutinous rice
  • 100g (1/2 cup) white glutinous rice
  • 150ml coconut milk
  • 2 TBSP (30g) sugar
  • 1 mango
  • Mint for garnish
  • 1tsp potato / corn starch mix with ¼ cup water for thickening

Directions:

  1. Wash the white and black glutinous rice in separate bowl. Drain.
  2. Put 1 cup of water in the black glutinous rice and ½ cup of water in the white glutinous rice.
  3. Soak for 4-6 hours, or overnight.
  4. Heat water in a steamer big enough to hold both the bowls. When the water boiled, put the glutinous rice into the steamer and steam for 30 min on medium flame.
  5. Put the coconut and sugar in a small saucepan and bring to boil. Stir often.
  6. Put 2 TBSP of sweetened coconut milk into each of the steamed rice, mix well and let cool.
  7. Thicken the remaining coconut milk with cornstarch water until desired thickness.
  8. Remove the skin and the pit of the mango and slice the meat to bite size.
  9. Arrange the black, white glutinous rice and mango onto a severing plate. Pour on the coconut sauce and garnish with mint.

Note:

  1. Feel free to be an artist and be creative with the decoration. I have seen it served as sushi style.
#10 - SAVORY CREPE WITH BACON - 煙肉雞蛋攤餅 This is a Chinese adaption of French food. Believe it or not, I used to eat it when I was a child in China. We did not have bacon or ham back, so we used crispy pork lard (the residue from making lard). It was delicious.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 2 cup Flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 ½ cup water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • White pepper
  • 4 pc bacon / 2pc ham
  • 4 green onion

Directions:

  1. Mix flour, salt and pepper in a mixing bowl.
  2. Crack the eggs in another bowl. Beat it with a fork, and then add the water.
  3. Fry the bacon until crisp. After it cools, and chop into small pieces.
  4. Wash and cut the green onion into small pieces.
  5. Slowly add the egg water mixture into the flour, stir constantly to avoid lumps to form.
  6. Mix the flour-egg mixture until smooth. (it there are lumps, pass the mixture through a sieve)
  7. Add the bacon and green onion and stir.
  8. Heat an 8 to 10-inch flat non-stick frying pan. Brush oil to cover the whole frying pan.
  9. With one hand holding the handle of the frying pan, pout about ¼ cup of the batter into the frying pan. Turn the frying pan in circular motion while adding the batter. The goal here is to cover the frying pan with a thin layer of batter. Add a bit more batter at the area not covered by the batter.
  10. Cook for 1 min until the batter at the edge of the frying pan starts to curl.
  11. Using a spatula, carefully lift up the crepe from the edge. Move the spatula under the crepe until the whole crepe separates from the frying pan.
  12. Lift up the crepe and flip it.
  13. Cook for another 30 sec until the underside has small brown spots.
  14. Remove the crepe and stack them on a flat plate.
  15. Repeat step 8-14 until all batter finsihes.

Note:

  1. These crepes are good for breakfast or lunch.
  2. Can also add other ingredients to make it more hearty and substantial meal (cooked sausage, sliced onion, pepper, mushroom, corn etc.)
#11 - STIR-FRIED BEEF WITH PEKING SCALLION - 京蔥爆牛肉 This is a famous Pekingese dish. Beef or lamb tenderloin slices are commonly used. I use the pre-sliced hot pot beef for convenience. You may also use chicken if preferred.

INGREDIENTS:

  • ½ lb thinly sliced premium beef (肥牛)
  • 1 large Peking scallion
  • ¼ red bell pepper
  • 2 slices ginger

MARINADE:

  • 1tsp dark soy sauce
  • 1tsp potato / corn starch
  • Pinch of baking soda
  • 2-4 TBSP water
  • Pinch of salt

SEASONING:

  • 1 TBSP cooking wine
  • 2 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp potato / corn starch mix with ¼ cup water for thickening
  • ½ tsp sesame oil

Directions:

  1. Cut the long strip of beef into shorter pieces.
  2. Marinate the beef and set aside.
  3. Wash and slice Peking scallion diagonally.
  4. Cut the red pepper into similar sizes.
  5. Heat 1 TBSP oil in a frying pan.
  6. When the oil is hot, put in the scallion and stir-fry until it just wilted.
  7. Put it in a bowl and set aside.
  8. Clean the frying pan; heat another 1 TBSP oil in it.
  9. Fry ginger until the aroma comes out. Remove the ginger and add in the beef.
  10. Stir-fry the beef until most of the beef has turn brown, but still have a bit tint of redness in some.
  11. Add in the cooking wine first, then the light soy, sugar and oyster sauce. Mix well.
  12. Finally add in the red bell pepper, the scallion. Stir everything together.
  13. Add sesame oil and serve.

Note:

  1. The baking soda in the marinade is to tenderize the meat. You may omit it if you want.
#12 - BRAISED BEEF BISKET - 紅燒牛腩 This is a very popular Cantonese dish. It is usually served on top of a bowl of soup noodle, or with some steaming white rice. A plate of sauté green vegetable is a perfect side dish for this meal. I like to cook this dish when I have time and keep it in the refrigerator for a quick meal in the next few days.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 600g（1斤）beef brisket牛腩
  • 600g beef tendon牛筋 (optional)
  • 1pc（1 cubic inch) ginger
  • 2 TBSP dark soy sauce
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 star anise
  • 1 pc cinnamon stick (桂皮）
  • 1 Chinese white turnip (optional)

Directions:

  1. Cut meat into 1.5-inch cube. Cut the beef tendon a bit bigger.
  2. Peel the skin of the turnip with a peeler and cut it into chunks about the size of the meat. Set aside for later.
  3. Bring a pot of water to boil and blanch the meat and tendon for 2 miniutes.
  4. Rinse in running water to remove scum.
  5. Put all ingredients into a crock-pot.
  6. Add water into the crock-pot to cover the meat.
  7. Bring to boil (it may take 1 hour). Let it cook until meat is tender (depending of the crock-pot, it may take a few hours. At least you don’t have to watch it)
  8. Remove the tendon from the pot when it is soft. Continue to cook the brisket until soft.
  9. Remove the cooked brisket from the pot.
  10. Transfer the liquid into a saucepan and condense the sauce to about a cup.
  11. Add the cut urnip into the sauce at this point and cook until tender (translucent).

Note:

  1. It is easiest to cook this dish in a crock-pot if you have one. But if you don’t’ have, it is OK too. Just cook it in a big pot with more water to cover the meat. Bring to boil, and then simmer for 1-2 hours. Check the water level and the doneness of the meat every 30 min. The rest of the procedures are the same.
#13 - SHANGHAINESE VEGETABLE RICE - 菜飯 Vegetable rice is commonly served with a Shanghainese meal. Typically, medium (shorter and fatter) grain rice is used. The cooked medium grain rice is more moist and tender than the long grain rice.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 ½ cup (2 rice cup) medium grain rice 珍珠米
  • 200g Shanghai vegetable (小棠菜)
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 cup chicken broth / water
  • 2 TBSP vegetable oil
  • Salted pork (optional) about 2 x 2 inches size

Directions:

  1. Wash, dry and cut vegetable into small pieces, separating the stalk and the green leaves.
  2. Crush the garlic with the flat part of the knife. Remove the skin.
  3. Heat a frying pan with 2 TBSP oil. When the oil is hot, put in the garlic, fry until slightly brown.
  4. Remove the garlic from oil and add in the vegetable stalk.
  5. Fry the vegetable stalk for a minute, then add in the leave.
  6. Cook until the leaves are wilted. Remove and set aside.
  7. Wash the rice, drain and put it in rice cooker. Add the water/chicken broth.
  8. Let the rice cook until it is bubbly and the water is almost absorbed.
  9. Put the vegetable in and let it cook until the rice is done.
  10. Keep warm until serving.

Note:

  1. Vegetable rice with salted pork:
    1. Cut salted pork into small pieces.
    2. Stir-fry salted pork, and then add it to the washed rice before turn on the rice cooker.
  2. Water-Rice ratio in this recipe is less than the usual rice cooking instruction because the vegetable will give out water.
#14 - ZUCCHINI PORK PIE - 翠玉瓜肉末扣餅 This the Chinese version of the stuffed pizza pie.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 cup (120g) All-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup (60ml) very warm (60ºC) water
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • 120 g Minced pork
  • 1 (80g) zucchini
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Minced pork seasoning:

  • 1 tsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tsp cooking wine
  • ¼ tsp Sugar
  • ½ tsp Salt
  • ½ TBSP Potato starch
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • pepper

Directions:

  1. Put the flour and ¼ tsp salt in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Add the water. Mix and knead the dough to form a ball. Rest for 15 minutes.
  3. Mix the minced pork with all the seasonings.
  4. Using chopsticks, stir the minced meat vigorously in circular motion until it becomes sticky pasty consistence.
  5. Wash and shred the zucchini with a shredder.
  6. Put the shredded zucchini in a muslin cloth and squeeze some excessive water from the zucchini.
  7. Add the zucchini into the minced pork and mix well. Roughly, divide into 10 equal portions by drawing lines in the meat stuffing.
  8. Divide the dough into 10 equal pieces.
  9. Using a rolling pin, roll each portion into a 4-inch circle.
  10. Put one portion of the meat stuffing onto a piece of the dough. Spread the stuffing evenly, leaving about ½ inch edge around.
  11. Put another piece of the dough on top. Press the middle gently to remove air pocket. Press the edge firmly.
  12. Using the edge of a bowl, press hardly along the circumference of the pie, cutting off ¼ inch of the excessive dough from the outer edge of the dough and form a nice round pie.
  13. Repeat steps 10 – 12 for the rest of the dough and meat stuffing.
  14. Heat a frying pan (preferably cast iron) with 2 TBSP oil. When the oil is hot, line the pie in a single layer on the frying pan without touching each other.
  15. With medium high heat, cook until the bottom side of the pie to light brown.
  16. Turn the pies over, add ½ cup of water in the pan and immediately cover.
  17. Let it cook until the water in the frying pan almost evaporated.
  18. Open the lid and let the moisture further evaporate and both sides golden brown.
  19. Remove and serve hot.

Note:

  1. Serve with your favorite hot sauce, Zhenjiang vinegar鎮江醋, or Worcestershire sauce.
#15 - FIVE SPICED BEEF SHANK - 五香牛蹍 This is another signature Shanghainese dish. My Mom made it well and was always loved by her guests. This is commonly served as one of the old appetizers before the main dishes.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 whole beef shank (1 ½ - 2 lb)
  • 1 tsp peppercorn
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 TBST dark soy sauce
  • 2 TBSP Shao Xing wine
  • 1tsp salt
  • 1 inch3 rock sugar
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 1 green onion

Directions:

  1. Rinse the beef shank in cold water.
  2. Bring a pot of water to boil and blanch the beef shank for 2 minutes.
  3. Rinse it in running water to remove the scum.
  4. Put the beef, and all the rest of the ingredients in a crook pot.
  5. Add water into the crock-pot to cover the meat.
  6. Bring to boil (it may take 1 hour). Let it cook until meat is tender. (Depending on the crock-pot, it may take 4 hours or more. At least you don’t have to keep an eye on it.)
  7. The meat is done when it feels soft when poke with a chopstick.
  8. Take the meat out and put it in a plastic box with a lid.
  9. Let it cool to room temperature, and then into the refrigerator for overnight.
  10. Put the sauce into a big jar and then into a refrigerator to let the fat solidify.
  11. Before serving, take out the sauce. Remove the fat from the top (it should be a layer of white solid on top of the brown sauce).
  12. Put the sauce in a saucepan to condense to ½ cup.
  13. Slice the beef out from the refrigerator, and slice it into thin slices.
  14. Put some condensed sauce on top. Serve cold.

Note:

  1. The leftover stays well in the refrigerator for a few days. It is nice to serve it with a bowl of soup noodle with some green vegetables on the side.
  2. Another serving suggestion is to put it inside the steam bread (see steam bread recipe).
#16 - DRUNKEN CHICKEN花 - 雕醉雞 This is an authentic Shanghainese dish as well as my mother’s signature dish. It is a must for her dinner parties and the guests loved it. It is better to use Huadiao 花雕 (the aged and better quality of Shaoxing wine) 紹興酒. This will give the chicken a strong wine fragrant and mellow taste. This dish is served as one of the many appetizers for a Shanghainese banquet and can prepared 1-2 days ahead of the time.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 whole chicken (approx. 2 pounds)
  • 2 cups Haodiao花雕
  • 6 TBSP sugar
  • 1 green onion
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Clean and pat dry chicken with paper towel.
  2. Rub inside and outside of chicken with salt and pepper.
  3. Put 2 slices of ginger and 1 green onion in the cavity.
  4. Steam chicken for 45 minutes or until well cooked. Dip into cold water to stop cooking.
  5. When the chicken cooled enough to handle, cut it into quarters.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, mix wine, sugar, salt and one green onion for marinating sauce.
  7. Submerge the chicken pieces in the marinating sauce.
  8. Marinate it for 1 day in the refrigerator. Turn chicken from time to time to submerge into the sauce.
  9. Take the chicken out of the marinade and cut into pieces. Arrange onto serving plate.
  10. Pour some marinating sauce on the chicken and serve at room.

Note:

  1. To test if the chicken is properly cooked, poke a chopstick into the thigh. It is done if and the juice comes out has no red color.
  2. If you can substitute whole chicken with 2 pounds of chicken wings or other cuts of chicken. Steaming time for chicken wings will be 20 minutes.
  3. You may use Shaoxing wine to substitute Huadiao.
#17 - CHILLED TOFU WITH CENTURY EGG - 涼拌皮蛋豆腐 Century egg is duck egg that is preserved in clay, salt and rice husk for a few weeks to months. The egg whites turns to dark jelly like and the yolk is grey creamy and succulent. It is a Chinese delicacy that many foreigners do not have the stomach for it. #37 - NEW YEAR CAKE (NIANGAO) - 糖年糕 This the most popular Chinese New Year food. You can find it everywhere before the Chinese New Year. This homemade version uses only a few ingredients and is not that hard to make.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 500 g glutinous rice flour
  • 100 g wheat starch
  • 400 g slab sugar (片糖)
  • 600 ml (1 ¼ cup) water
  • 3 TBSP vegetable oil
  • ½ piece red date for garnish
  • 1-2 eggs
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Directions:

  1. Put the slab sugar and water in a saucepan. Bring to boil and simmer until all the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Let the sugar water cool to 60ºC
  3. Mix the glutinous rice flour and wheat starch and put it through a sieve into a big bowl.
  4. Pour the sugar water into the flour mixture slowly, stirring with a wooden spatula continually, trying to break up the lumps.
  5. Mix in vegetable oil.
  6. Pass the mixture through a sieve to make sure that there is no lumps in it.
  7. Oil an approx. 1200 ml circular container.
  8. Pour the mixture into a circular container, gently knock the container on the counter to make remove air bubbles and smooth out top surface.

  10. Heat a steamer big enough to hold the circular container.

  17. Fry on low heat, until both side of the rice cake is lightly brown.
  18. Serve.
